For food safety reasons, I was asked the question of what temperature is the interior of my refrigerator and does it exceed safety limits during warm weather? I want to make sure my fridge stays below 39F (or 4C - recommendations are 5C but I allow for measurement error)

Question: Is my Refrigerator Safe According to Food Safety Recommendations

During the summer of 2020, I wired and coded a Raspberry Pi Zero W with two sensors. I mean, the pandemic had many things closed down, and what else was there to do? One sensor detected temperature and relative humidity and the other just temperature (in degrees Celsius). I put the temperature-only sensor in my refrigerator and the dual-sensor outside of my refrigerator. After a week of readings, I downloaded the data file named temperatures.csv from my Raspberry Pi.

If current_date was a string (i.e.chr), I would need to convert it to a date. read_csv() did a good job identifying it as a datetime, but sometimes R gets confused and dates get read as characters.

fridge <- fridge %>% mutate(current_date = ymd_hms(current_date))

mutate() creates a new column based on some formula - in this case converting current_date to the datetime column current_date (yes, I used the same name and so it just overwrites it). current_date was already a datetime, but now we are sure. If current_date was a character string, it is a date now, or we got an error.

Note, the symbol %>% is a function in magrittr (part of the tidyverse package) that allows us to connect commands together into one big command. The function is meant to do the same thing as a pipe in Linux.

Conclusion

For the 7 days of the test (August 24 to September 1, 2020), the external temperature averaged 21C and the humidity was between 61% and 72%. Although the mean temperature of my refrigerator was 2.4C, it was out of compliance with food safety standards 14% of the time.

Note that I could conclude something about the temperature on certain days of the week (during the observation period, at least), the relationship between the external and internal temperatures, the relationship with external humidity, and the relationship with opening the refrigerator door (i.e.when Motion occurred). Although we found plenty of data during our exploration, our focus is the research question about the temperature range of my refrigerator.
